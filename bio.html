Bio

I am a PhD student in the Agricultural and Resource Economics (ARE) department at UC Berkeley. My research focuses on the global consequences of climate change for society. Using large geospatial datasets and econometric methods, I study how changing temperatures and economic development reshape electricity demand, freshwater availability, and agricultural systems worldwide.

Before Berkeley I was a Pre-Doctoral Research Fellow at the Environmental Markets Lab (emLab) at UC Santa Barbara, where I worked on projects at the intersection of climate change, water resource economics, and global hydrological risk. I completed a Master of Science in Environmental Economics and Climate Change at the London School of Economics (LSE) and hold bachelor's degrees in Econometrics and Economics from Erasmus University Rotterdam.
